For an operator $T$ with $Rank(T)=Rank(T^2)$ ,$\eta(T) \cap \rho(T)=\{0\}$

We have $T^2(V) \subset T(v)$ and $Rank(T^2)=Rank(T)$
$$\Rightarrow \rho(T^2)=\rho(T)$$
Then by Rank nullity Theorem $$\eta(T)=\eta(T^2)$$ Suppose $x \in \eta(T) \cap \rho(T) $ then $\exists x' \in V $ such that $$T(x')=x$$ and $$T^2(x')=T(T(x'))=T(x)=0(\text{since } x \in \eta(T))$$ $$\Rightarrow x' \in \eta(T^2)=\eta(T)$$ $$\Rightarrow T(x')=x=0$$
QED
